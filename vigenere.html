Vigenere Cipher

-A project by Sonas MacRae-

Encrypt your message

To use this cipher, enter some text into the "input" box and enter a keyword into the input box labelled "keyword", once you are satisfied with your choice of inputs go ahead and smack press the "encryption" button.. Your encrypted message should appear in the output box, press the "Copy output" button to save your secret message to your clipboard. All encrypted messages have spaces removed.
To decipher a message enter text into the input area and hit the button labelled "Decrypt". This cipher works by incrementing each letter of the input using a letter of the keyword in order, values of the keyword are reused if the input is longer than the key. If the keyword is only one character long then the encryption is essentially a Caesar cipher.

I want to know more

What is today known as the Vigenere Cipher was actually first described by Giovan Battista Bellaso in his 1553 book La cifra del. Sig. Giovan Battista Bellaso. However, in the 19th Century, it was misattributed to Blaise de Vigenere, who had presented a similar cipher (The Auto Key cipher) in 1586. At the time, and for many centuries since its invention, the Vigenere Cipher was renowned for being a very secure cipher, and for a very long time it was believed to be unbreakable. It was this thought that earned it the nickname "le chiffre indechiffrable" (French for "the unbreakable cipher"). Although this is not true (it was fully broken by Friedrich Kasiski in 1863), it is still a very secure cipher in terms of paper and pen methods, and is usable as a field cipher.
