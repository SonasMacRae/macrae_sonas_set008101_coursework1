Caesar Cipher

-A project by Sonas MacRae-

Encrypt some text

I chose Caesars Cipher as the first cipher for this webpage because I think it's a good starting point, it's simple enough to understand and interesting too. To use this cipher, type into the box labelled "Plain text" and use the drop down menu to choose your key to encrypt with, ('A' shifting the letters by 0 places and 'Z' shifting the letters by 25 places) press the button that says "Encrypt", the encrypted message should appear. Now that you have your secret message you can press the button labelled "copy" which will copy the encrypted text to your clipboard and can then be pasted back into the input box for decryption. To decrypt a message back to its original state, press the button labelled "Decrypt".

More about this cipher

In cryptography, a Caesar cipher, also known as Caesar's cipher, the shift cipher, Caesar's code or Caesar shift, is one of the simplest and most widely known encryption techniques. It is a type of substitution cipher in which each letter in the plaintext is replaced by a letter some fixed number of positions down the alphabet. For example, with a left shift of 3, D would be replaced by A, E would become B, and so on. The method is named after Julius Caesar, who used it in his private correspondence.[1] The encryption step performed by a Caesar cipher is often incorporated as part of more complex schemes, such as the Vigen√®re cipher, and still has modern application in the ROT13 system. As with all single-alphabet substitution ciphers, the Caesar cipher is easily broken and in modern practice offers essentially no communication security.
